The country is one of the most affected by climate changes, most seen by the incessant drying of the Aral Sea, with over half of its area dried up, something devastating for a country which is already double- landlocked. This is also combined with increasing temperatures, droughts and shifts in weather and precipitation patterns, which impact agriculture.​The problem is magnified by the incessant drying of the Aral Sea, which has already lost ***57 percent*** of its area, **80 percent** of its volume and **64 percent** of its depth over the past four decades."the transition to a green economy and achieving carbon neutrality are a priority strategic taskof the New Uzbekistan.”  - President Sh.
